Pihlajasaari Recreational Park

At Pihlajasaari Recreational Park you can swim at sandy beaches or jog and hike
through wooded areas. This island was once a villa resort with small cottages scattered
throughout its forests and seafronts. Today the 26 hectare (64 acre)
site attracts sun worshipers and active vacationers from all over the country.
Visit the beach by boat and spend a day surrounded by diverse wildlife and
colorful cottages, or swim in the clear sea.
You can also camp at a designated area. If you're hungry, visit the restaurant inside
a 19th-century wooden villa for some smoked Finnish salmon.
Our Helsinki trip itinerary planner makes visiting Pihlajasaari Recreational Park
and other Helsinki attractions simple, and helps you make a travel plan personal to you.

Ruoholahti Canal

The 1990's saw a number of projects start in Helsinki
where former industrial seafront areas
have been redeveloped for residential use. One of these was
the Ruoholahti district in the western part of central Helsinki,
where a new canal was constructed through
the residential area. The shorelines of the Ruoholahti canal
have been lit at a relatively low level,
creating a pleasant and light environment.
The residential buildings have not been the focus,
but they form a background that defines the area.
Care has also been taken to ensure sufficient lightingB
for safe access to the boats.

Vallisaari

You will get to Vallisaari Island by
water buss during the summer period May-September 2017.
The water buss transportation providers are JT-line
and Aavalines and transportation starts from the Market Square.
The departure point to Vallisaari is from beside
the ferryboat departing to Suomenlinna Island.
At Vallisaari the water busses arrive to
Torpedonlahti harbor square. At the Market Square
you will find the departure point beside the "Vallisaari" sign.
In addition to its beautiful scenery
and fascinating history, Vallisaari boasts
a range of services for tourists: water points, dry toilets
and during summer season also an excursion harbour,
a cafe, and a tour guide service.
It is allowed to berth at excursion harbor year round.
Please note, that
there is no winter maintenance on the trails.
Dogs are welcome, but must be kept on a lead.
